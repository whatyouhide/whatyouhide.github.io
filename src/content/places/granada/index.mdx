Granada sits at the foot of the Sierra Nevada, with the Alhambra perched above the city like a deep-red crown. The name itself means *pomegranate* — and the fruit is everywhere, from the city's coat of arms to the little brass studs embedded in the sidewalks of the old town.

## First Impressions

We arrived in the late afternoon. The light here is unlike anything else — warm, almost amber, washing over the white-and-ochre walls of the Albaicín. Everything feels old and lived-in.

## Food

Tapas in Granada are still *free* with a drink, which is wild. You order a beer and a small plate of jamón, croquetas, or patatas bravas just shows up. We ate our way across the city this way.

## Alhambra

You need to book tickets weeks in advance, and even then it's the most-crowded place in town. Worth every second.
